What is the Surcharge Violation System in New Jersey, and How Does it Work?

New Jersey has a unique system in place to penalize drivers who accumulate certain traffic violations or points on their driving record. This system, known as the surcharge violation system, imposes additional financial penalties on top of standard fines for traffic offenses. If you are a driver in New Jersey, understanding how this system works is essential to avoiding unnecessary penalties and fees.

How the Surcharge Violation System Works
The surcharge violation system is designed to impose recurring fines on drivers who have committed serious traffic offenses or have accumulated excessive points on their driving record. Unlike standard tickets or one-time fines, these surcharges must be paid on an annual basis for a set number of years, depending on the type of violation.

Drivers who receive certain violations, such as driving under the influence (DUI) or driving without insurance, automatically enter the surcharge violation system. Additionally, any driver who accumulates six or more points on their license within three years will also be subject to surcharges. These fees are separate from court fines and are due directly to the New Jersey Motor Vehicle Commission (MVC).

Types of Violations That Lead to Surcharges
There are several infractions that can result in being placed into the surcharge violation system. Some of the most common include:

DUI or refusal to take a breathalyzer test: Drivers convicted of DUI face an annual surcharge for three consecutive years.
Driving without proper insurance: A driver caught operating a vehicle without insurance may face a recurring surcharge for up to three years.

Accumulation of six or more points: If a driver accumulates six or more points on their record within three years, they will be required to pay a surcharge in addition to any standard fines.

In these cases, the surcharge violation system adds another layer of financial responsibility, making it more expensive for repeat offenders to continue driving without addressing their driving habits.

Payment and Consequences for Non-Payment
Once a driver has been notified that they owe surcharges, payments must be made to the New Jersey MVC. Payments can often be made as a lump sum or in installment plans, depending on eligibility. Failure to pay surcharges can lead to severe consequences, including:

Suspension of driving privileges

Additional penalties and late fees

Involvement of collection agencies

Potential legal action
Since the surcharge violation system operates separately from court fines, it is important for drivers to stay informed about any surcharges they may owe to prevent unexpected license suspensions.

How to Avoid Surcharge Violations
The best way to avoid being penalized by the surcharge violation system is to practice safe and responsible driving. By following traffic laws, obeying speed limits, and avoiding reckless behaviors, drivers can keep their point totals low and reduce the risk of surcharges. Additionally, New Jersey offers defensive driving courses that allow drivers to remove points from their records, which can help them stay below the six-point threshold.

Drivers should also ensure that they maintain proper insurance coverage and always renew their policies on time to avoid being charged with driving without insurance, a violation that leads to an automatic surcharge.

Conclusion
Understanding the surcharge violation system in New Jersey is crucial for drivers who want to avoid costly penalties. This system applies additional financial burdens to those who commit serious traffic violations or accumulate too many points on their driving record. Since failure to pay surcharges can result in license suspension and other consequences, staying informed and managing driving habits responsibly is key. By driving safely and maintaining proper insurance, New Jersey drivers can minimize their risk of facing these costly penalties.
