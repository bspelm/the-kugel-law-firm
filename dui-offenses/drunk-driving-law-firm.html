Consequences of Drunk Driving in New Jersey

The penalties for drunk driving are severe in New Jersey and can affect your reputation, your job, your ability to drive, your insurance rates, and other factors that impact your daily life. A DWI is a serious traffic offense in New Jersey and carries with it significant jail time, fines, license suspension, community service, alcohol interlock device requirement, and other negative consequences that can have a lasting effect on your life. DWI penalties in New Jersey vary depending on whether it is your first, second, or subsequent offense. First offenses are generally not treated as harshly as later offenses. However, even for first-time offenders, consulting with a skilled drunk driving law firm can help you understand your rights and options for minimizing the impact of the penalties.

For example, the first time you are convicted of DWI in New Jersey, your license will be suspended for three to twelve months; you will be required to attend the Intoxicated Driver Resource Center (IDRC); you will spend up to 30 days in jail; you will face substantial fines and fees; you may have your vehicle impounded or towed; you will be required to install an ignition interlock device in your car for two to four years; you will have a mandatory jail sentence of 48 hours, and you will also likely have a three-year surcharge on your auto insurance premium.

If you are a repeat offender, the penalties will increase significantly. For a second offense, the fines double, you will have to serve up to 180 days in jail; you will have to attend the IDRC for a longer period of time; your license will be suspended for up to two years; and you will be required to install an ignition interlock in your car for three to five years. Having the guidance of an experienced drunk driving law firm can be invaluable when facing these elevated penalties.

You should also be aware that a DUI conviction in New Jersey can have consequences in other states. This is because New Jersey is a member of the Interstate Driver’s License Compact, which allows states to share information regarding DUI convictions with other states. As a result, if you are arrested for DUI in New Jersey and have an out-of-state license, your home state may revoke your privileges and penalize you accordingly.
